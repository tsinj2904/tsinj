Privacy Policy
Last Updated: March 2026
Section 1
Our Commitment to Your Privacy

Success Institute is a 501(c)(3) nonprofit organization dedicated to building foundational habits in students. EIN: 85-1642627. We value the trust you place in us and are committed to protecting the privacy of our students and their families.

Section 2
Information We Collect

We collect information that you voluntarily provide to us when you register for a workshop, apply for a cohort, or contact us. This may include:

  • Parent/Guardian Information: Name, email address, and phone number.
  • Student Information: Name and current grade level.
  • Application Details: Brief insights into why our program resonates with your family.
Section 3
Use of Third-Party Educational Tools

To provide a high-quality, tech-forward learning experience, we utilize the following platforms. Each has its own rigorous privacy standards:

  • Google Workspace for Education / Google Classroom: We use this for session recaps and resource sharing. Google does not own school data or sell it to third parties.
  • StudyFetch: We use this AI-driven platform to create interactive study materials. StudyFetch does not display third-party advertisements.
Section 4
Google Ads and Analytics Disclosures

As a participant in the Google Ad Grant program, we use Google Ads and Google Analytics to understand how visitors interact with our site.

  • Cookies: We use cookies to serve ads based on past visits to our website.
  • Opt-Out: You can opt out of Google's use of cookies by visiting Google's Ad Settings or the Network Advertising Initiative opt-out page.
Section 5
Data Protection and Sharing

We do not sell, rent, or lease your personal information to third parties. Access to student data is strictly limited to Dr. Rick Alonso and authorized Success Institute staff for educational purposes only.

Section 6
Children's Privacy (COPPA)

Our programs serve students in grades K–12. We do not knowingly collect personal information directly from children under 13 without explicit parental consent provided during the application process. Parents have the right to review or request the deletion of their child's information at any time.
