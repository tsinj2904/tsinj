Sound Familiar?

Your child is bright.
So why is staying on task so hard?

Every family shares the same gap — whether in Caldwell or East Orange. School teaches the what. Reading, math, science, history. But who teaches the how? How to focus when it is hard. How to pause before reacting. How to recover when things do not go as planned. How to think through a problem independently rather than waiting to be told what to do next.

These are not personality traits. They are skills. And like any skill, they can be taught, practiced, and built. That is exactly what Success Institute exists to do.

Parents often tell us their child...
✓Has trouble staying focused on one thing at a time
✓Reacts before thinking, then regrets it
✓Gets frustrated quickly when something is hard
✓Struggles to follow multi-step directions independently
✓Needs constant prompting to start or stay on a task
✓Is clearly capable, but not performing to potential

If any of these sound familiar, your child is not struggling. They are simply missing skills that were never explicitly taught.

Our Philosophy

Internal capability determines
long-term success.

Every day, students are taught what to learn. Far less time is devoted to strengthening the internal skills that determine how well they learn, and how well they perform across every subject, relationship, and challenge they will ever face.

"At Success Institute, we believe these internal capabilities determine how well a student performs. Not just today, but through middle school, high school, and beyond. We exist to develop them intentionally and measurably."
— Dr. Ricardo Alonso, Ed.D. · Founder & Executive Director

Program Structure

12 weeks. One hour per Saturday.
Three progressive phases.

Weeks 1–4
Build Core Habits

Establish the foundational attention and listening habits that all future learning depends on.

Focus and attention stamina Listening for key details Basic pause-and-think Entry rituals and expectations
Weeks 5–8
Layer Emotional Regulation

Introduce structured emotional recovery and early reasoning frameworks students can use independently.

Frustration tolerance Stop-Think-Choose framework Priority decision-making Explaining reasoning clearly
Weeks 9–12
Integrate and Apply

Apply all capabilities in small group challenges that prepare students for the Formation Track ahead.

Group decision-making Planning one step ahead Handling disappointment well Documenting personal growth
